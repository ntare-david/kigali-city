City Administration

Kigali is led by a Mayor and a City Council that oversees the development and management of the city. The current administration has implemented numerous initiatives to improve infrastructure, promote sustainable development, and enhance the quality of life for residents.

The city's leadership has been instrumental in Kigali's transformation into a model African city, with a focus on cleanliness, security, and modern urban planning. Their vision includes making Kigali a hub for business, technology, and tourism in East Africa.

Regular town hall meetings and community engagement programs ensure that citizens have a voice in the city's development, fostering a sense of ownership and pride among Kigali's residents.

Kigali City

Explore the three administrative districts that make up Rwanda's capital

Gasabo District

The largest district in Kigali, home to government institutions, upscale residential areas, and the Kigali Convention Centre. Gasabo includes neighborhoods like Kimihurura, Remera, and Kacyiru.

Kicukiro District

Located in the southeastern part of Kigali, Kicukiro is known for its industrial zones, educational institutions, and residential areas. It includes neighborhoods like Gikondo, Kagarama, and Niboye.

Nyarugenge District

The central business district of Kigali, featuring commercial centers, financial institutions, and the historic Nyamirambo neighborhood. It's the economic heart of the city.

Parks and Attractions

Discover the natural beauty and cultural landmarks in and around Kigali

Kigali Genocide Memorial

The Kigali Genocide Memorial is a powerful and moving tribute to the victims of the 1994 genocide. This important site serves as both a place of remembrance and an educational center, helping visitors understand Rwanda's tragic past and its remarkable journey of reconciliation and healing.

The memorial includes exhibits that document the history leading up to the genocide, personal stories of victims and survivors, and Rwanda's path to recovery. It's an essential visit for anyone seeking to understand contemporary Rwanda.

Kandt House Museum

Kandt House Museum is a historic and cultural landmark located in Kigali, Rwanda. Originally built in 1908 as the home of Dr. Richard Kandt, the first German colonial resident, it is the oldest colonial-era building in the country. Now a museum, it showcases Rwanda's journey through three key periods: pre-colonial life, the colonial era, and the development of modern Kigali. The museum also features exhibits on Rwanda's natural history, including live reptiles and displays about the country's biodiversity. It offers visitors a rich insight into Rwanda's heritage, culture, and urban evolution.

Nyandungu Eco-Park

Nyandungu Eco-Park is a restored wetland eco-park located in Kigali, Rwanda. It offers a peaceful escape with walking trails, cycling paths, birdwatching spots, and lush greenery. The park promotes conservation, eco-tourism, and education, making it a must-visit destination for nature lovers and visitors to Kigali.
